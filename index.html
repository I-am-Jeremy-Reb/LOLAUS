▸ How the centroid is calculated▼

The intensity-weighted centroid locates the beam's centre of mass:

\(\bar{x}=\dfrac{\sum x\,I(x,y)}{\sum I},\qquad\bar{y}=\dfrac{\sum y\,I(x,y)}{\sum I}\)

Only pixels above 10% of the peak contribute, preventing read-out noise from biasing the result. The camera noise floor (histogram peak in the low-level range) is subtracted beforehand, and saturated pixels are replaced by the 5×5 neighbourhood median. The centroid is sub-pixel accurate.

▸ How the Strehl ratio is calculated▼

The Strehl ratio S is the ratio of the intensity between an abberated and idealized focal spot. S = I_measured/I_Diffraction-Limited. S = 1 is the maximum corrseponding to a perfectly focused diffraction limited spot.

\[S = \frac{I_{\text{measured}}}{I_{\text{DL}}}= \frac{P/A_{\text{measured}}}{P/A_{\text{DL}}}= \frac{P}{\pi r_m^2} \Big/ \frac{P}{\pi r_{\text{DL}}^2}= \left(\frac{r_{\text{DL}}}{r_m}\right)^2\]

rDL is the theoretical diffraction-limited 86.5% EE radius you supply; r₈₆.₅ is the measured radius. For a fixed total energy, peak intensity scales as 1/r², so the ratio of peak intensities equals the inverse square of the radii. This approximation is most accurate for nearly-Gaussian beams with mild aberrations (S ≳ 0.3).

▸ How ellipticity is calculated▼

Ellipticity uses the second-moment (variance–covariance) tensor of the intensity:

\(\sigma_{xx}=\dfrac{\sum(x-\bar x)^2 I}{\sum I},\quad\sigma_{yy}=\dfrac{\sum(y-\bar y)^2 I}{\sum I},\quad\sigma_{xy}=\dfrac{\sum(x-\bar x)(y-\bar y)I}{\sum I}\)

The eigenvalues \(\lambda_{1,2}\) of \(\bigl(\begin{smallmatrix}\sigma_{xx}&\sigma_{xy}\\\sigma_{xy}&\sigma_{yy}\end{smallmatrix}\bigr)\) give the principal-axis variances. The 1/e² semi-axes are \(w_{1,2}=2\sqrt{\lambda_{1,2}}\) and \(\varepsilon = w_\text{min}/w_\text{max}\in[0,1]\). A perfect circular beam gives ε = 1. Values ≥ 0.87 are generally acceptable; below 0.70 indicates significant astigmatism. The orientation angle θ of the major axis (degrees from horizontal) is computed from the dominant eigenvector.

The yellow dashed ellipse overlaid on the image represents the 1/e² principal-axis ellipse derived from the second moments.

Peak Intensity & Normalised Vector Potential a₀

\( I_0 = \dfrac{E}{\tau\,\pi\,r_{86.5}^2} \qquad a_0 = \sqrt{\dfrac{I_0\,\lambda_{\mu\text{m}}^2}{1.37\times10^{18}}} \)

Peak intensity \(I_0\) [W/cm²] for a spatially and temporally Gaussian pulse of energy \(E\) [J], FWHM duration τ [fs], and 1/e² radius \(r_{86.5}\) [Microns]. \(a_0\) uses λ in Microns; the constant 1.37×10¹⁸ W/cm²/Microns² is the relativistic intensity threshold for λ = 1 Microns.
